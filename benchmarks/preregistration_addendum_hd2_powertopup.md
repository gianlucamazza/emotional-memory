## Motivation

From `preregistration_addendum_hd2_closure.md`:

- **Hd2_IT (me5, N=80):** Δ +0.1625 [0.0375, 0.2875], p_raw=0.0118 / p_bonf×5=0.060 (borderline FAIL at family level), d=0.290.
- **Hd2_ES.me5 (N=80):** Δ +0.1125 [-0.0125, 0.2375], p=0.1095 (FAIL), d=0.189.
- The closure explicitly states: *"d=0.189 requires N≈120 for 80% power"*.

The paper §6 cites cross-language replication as supporting evidence for the headline AFT effect.
Running at N=80 when the power calculation declares N=120 leaves the claim vulnerable to the
obvious objection: "you declared power for N=120 and stopped early." This addendum runs the
pre-declared power target and reports the honest result.

**Three possible outcomes (branches A/B/C) are pre-declared below. All three are publishable;
the paper update depends on which branch materialises.**

### Hd2_IT_v120

> On `realistic_recall_v2_it.json` extended to N=120 (30 scenarios × 4 queries, embedder
> `multilingual-e5-small`, top_k=2, oracle-affect mode), AFT `top1_accuracy` >

### Hd2_ES_v120

> On `realistic_recall_v2_es.json` extended to N=120 (same configuration), AFT

Both are one-tailed (directional): the sign of Δ was already observed at N=80 and the

## Dataset extension method (generation protocol)

Datasets are extended from 20 → 30 scenarios (+10 per language). This section is committed
**before** dataset generation to prevent data-snooping.

### Italian scenarios (21–30)

Generated via `scripts/generate_realistic_v2_topup.py` with the following constraints:

1. **LLM-assisted text generation (gpt-4.1-mini):** The LLM generates scenario narrative,
   event text (`content`), `metadata.topic`/`metadata.phase`, and query text. It does NOT
   assign valence or arousal values.

2. **Programmatic valence/arousal assignment:** Values are assigned from a per-`challenge_type`
   template (see below) derived from the existing 20 scenarios. The LLM has no influence
   over the numerical affect signal.

3. **Balanced challenge_type distribution:** The 10 new scenarios × 4 queries/scenario = 40
   new queries must distribute 8 per challenge_type (each of the 5 types gets exactly 8 new
   queries), achieved by cycling a 5-type rotation across 10 scenarios.

4. **Few-shot conditioning:** The LLM prompt includes one representative existing scenario
   per challenge_type as a few-shot example, conditioning on structure and style without
   revealing the outcome of the N=80 run.

5. **ID unicity:** Scenario IDs continue `_21` through `_30`. Memory IDs are checked for
   global uniqueness against all existing IDs before commit.

6. **Determinism:** `seed=42`, model=`gpt-4.1-mini`. LLM call provenance recorded in
   `benchmarks/datasets/realistic_recall_v2_topup_provenance.jsonl`.

### Valence/arousal templates per challenge_type

(Derived from the mean structure of existing 20 scenarios, rounded for simplicity)

| challenge_type | Sessions 1 events pattern | Session 2 events pattern | Query state |
|---|---|---|---|
| `affective_arc` | strong negative → weak positive (v: −0.8…−0.5, a: 0.8…0.5) | moderate → strong positive (v: +0.7…+0.95, a: 0.6…0.85) | v=+0.85, a=0.75 |
| `recency_confound` | negative → neutral → positive target; recent event = neutral/weak-positive | recent event: v=+0.1..+0.2, a=0.3; target: v=+0.65..+0.80, a=0.50 | v=+0.6, a=0.4 |
| `same_topic_distractor` | mixed trajectory; two events close semantically: one v<0, one v>0.8 | culmination positive | v=+0.9, a=0.75 |
| `semantic_confound` | early strong negative event (target); later positive with similar keywords | similar-topic positive event in session 2 | v=−0.9, a=0.95 |
| `momentum_alignment` | arousal increasing arc from low to high; culmination event strong positive | resolution + quiet follow-up | v=+0.9, a=0.85 |

### Spanish scenarios (21–30)

Generated by translating the 10 new Italian scenarios via the same LLM with a dedicated
translation prompt. Session IDs use `sesion_1`/`sesion_2`. Memory IDs use Spanish
equivalents (e.g., `trabajo_` prefix). Valence/arousal values are **identical** to the
Italian counterparts (translation does not modify the affect signal). Topic mapping follows
the same Spanish cultural adaptation pattern as the original 20 scenarios (commit `898e132`).

## Statistical handling

- **Primary metric:** `top1_accuracy` (same as all Hd2 closures).
- **Paired bootstrap:** n=10000, seed=0 (coerente con tutte le chiusure precedenti).
- **Family Holm correction:** m=2 (IT and ES as a single family of top-up replications).
- **One-tailed alternative** (AFT > cosine, directional, sign pre-established at N=80).
- **Report per language:** Δ, 95% CI (paired bootstrap), p_bootstrap (one-tailed),
  p_mcnemar, p_holm, d Cohen, n_discordant, per-challenge breakdown (5 types).
- **No new ablation families:** this addendum adds no new hypotheses beyond the two
  replication tests.

---

## Decision rule

**PASS (per language):** `p_holm < 0.05` AND `Δ > 0` AND 95% CI does not cross 0.

**FAIL (per language):** otherwise.

### Branch declarations

| Outcome | Verdict | Paper §6 action | Abstract action |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A (PASS-PASS)** | Cross-language effect replicates at declared power | Remove "borderline" caveat; update numbers to N=120 | Add "replicated cross-language at N=120" |
| **B (PASS-FAIL)** | Heterogeneous effect: Italian stable, Spanish does not reach significance | §6 reports both; notes "Spanish effect not established at N=120" | Only Italian cited as cross-language support; ES as limitation |
| **C (FAIL-FAIL)** | Effect does not survive power top-up | §6 reports both as FAIL at N=120; cross-language evidence not established | Remove or caveat cross-language claim; flag as limitation |

All three branches are honest and publishable. No post-hoc threshold adjustments are
permitted after observing results.
