    Bibliography¶

    Complete references for all sources cited in the research documentation. Organized by category with links where available.

    Philosophy¶

    Ancient Texts¶

    • Aristotle. De Anima (On the Soul). Trans. D.W. Hamlyn. Oxford: Clarendon Press, 1968.
    • Aristotle. De Memoria et Reminiscentia (On Memory and Recollection). In Parva Naturalia. Trans. J.I. Beare. Available: https://classics.mit.edu/Aristotle/memory.html
    • Plato. Meno. In Complete Works. Indianapolis: Hackett.
    • Plato. Phaedo. In Complete Works. Indianapolis: Hackett.
    • Plato. Phaedrus. In Complete Works. Indianapolis: Hackett.
    • Diogenes Laertius (source for the Stoics). Lives of the Eminent Philosophers, Book VII (Stoics).
    • Epictetus. Enchiridion. Trans. W.A. Oldfather. Cambridge: Harvard University Press, 1928.

    Early Modern Period¶

    • Descartes, R. (1649). Les Passions de l'âme. Paris.
    • Spinoza, B. (1677). Ethica Ordine Geometrico Demonstrata. Trans. E. Curley. Princeton: Princeton University Press, 1994.
    • Particularly: Part III (De Origine et Natura Affectuum), Propositions 18, 11–15.
    • Hume, D. (1739–40). A Treatise of Human Nature. Ed. L.A. Selby-Bigge. Oxford: Clarendon Press, 1978.

    Phenomenology¶

    • Husserl, E. (1893–1917/1928). Vorlesungen zur Phänomenologie des inneren Zeitbewusstseins. Ed. M. Heidegger. Halle: Niemeyer. Trans.: On the Phenomenology of the Consciousness of Internal Time. Dordrecht: Kluwer, 1991.
    • Husserl, E. (1900–01). Logische Untersuchungen. Trans.: Logical Investigations. London: Routledge, 2001.
    • Heidegger, M. (1927). Sein und Zeit. Tübingen: Niemeyer. Trans.: Being and Time. New York: Harper & Row, 1962.
    • Particularly: §29–34 (Die Befindlichkeit, Die Angst, Die Stimmung).
    • Heidegger, M. (1929–30). Die Grundbegriffe der Metaphysik. Frankfurt: Klostermann. Trans.: Fundamental Concepts of Metaphysics. Bloomington: Indiana University Press, 1995.
    • Merleau-Ponty, M. (1945). Phénoménologie de la perception. Paris: Gallimard. Trans.: Phenomenology of Perception. London: Routledge, 2002.

    Contemporary Philosophy¶

    • Clark, A. & Chalmers, D. (1998). "The Extended Mind." Analysis, 58(1), 7–19. Available: https://www.alice.id.tue.nl/references/clark-chalmers-1998.pdf
    • Clark, A. (2008). Supersizing the Mind: Embodiment, Action, and Cognitive Extension. Oxford: Oxford University Press.
    • Colombetti, G. (2014). The Feeling Body: Affective Science Meets the Enactive Mind. Cambridge: MIT Press. Available: https://direct.mit.edu/books/monograph/2211/
    • Varela, F., Thompson, E. & Rosch, E. (1991). The Embodied Mind: Cognitive Science and Human Experience. Cambridge: MIT Press.
    • Thompson, E. (2007). Mind in Life: Biology, Phenomenology, and the Sciences of Mind. Cambridge: Harvard University Press.
    • Turkle, S. (ed.) (2007). Evocative Objects: Things We Think With. Cambridge: MIT Press.

    Cognitive Appraisal¶

    • Lazarus, R.S. (1991). Emotion and Adaptation. New York: Oxford University Press.

    • Arnold, M.B. (1960). Emotion and Personality (2 vols). New York: Columbia University Press.

    Predictive Learning and Arousal¶

    • Pearce, J.M. & Hall, G. (1980). "A model for Pavlovian learning: Variations in the effectiveness of conditioned but not of unconditioned stimuli." Psychological Review, 87(6), 532–552. The Pearce-Hall predictor-error theory underlies the APE-gated reconsolidation mechanism: only events with high prediction error open the reconsolidation window.
    • Yerkes, R.M. & Dodson, J.D. (1908). "The relation of strength of stimulus to rapidity of habit-formation." Journal of Comparative Neurology and Psychology, 18(5), 459–482. The Yerkes-Dodson curve informs the non-linear relationship between arousal and consolidation strength: peak around moderate arousal, degradation at extremes.
