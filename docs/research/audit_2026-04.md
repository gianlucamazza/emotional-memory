# Audit: Studies & Research (April 2026)

A critical self-review of the AFT research corpus — strengths, gaps, and
priority order for closing the remaining evidence. Intended both as an internal
compass and as reviewer-anticipation prep for the arXiv submission.

---

## 1. Snapshot

AFT is shipped as a five-layer emotional memory library with a theory stack
(philosophy → neuroscience → computational models → design principles), 9
families of empirical studies, a pre-registration + 5 addenda (B/D/E/F/H),
and a canonical claim matrix. The public claim ceiling, per
[`10_scientific_quality_bar.md`](10_scientific_quality_bar.md), is currently
**"strong theory-driven prototype with early controlled evidence + one honest
external negative."** Of the six tracked claims in
[`claim_validation_matrix.json`](claim_validation_matrix.json), three have
controlled/early evidence, one carries strong intra-theory evidence, one is
`implemented` only, and one is `not_established`; none carry ecological
validation yet.

**Quality gate status (2026-05-02):**
- **Gate 1** (external benchmark): CLOSED — LoCoMo H1/H2 FAIL, honest negative result documented.
- **Gate 2** (human eval): OPEN — kit shipped (`benchmarks/human_eval/`), zero rater ratings collected.
- **Gate 3** (architecture attribution): CLOSED — Hd1 PASS (Addendum D), `aft_noAppraisal` Δ=+0.23 vs `naive_cosine`.

## 3. Strengths

- **Theory-fidelity density.** 126 cases across 20 phenomena in
  `benchmarks/fidelity/` directly validate Russell mood-congruence, Yerkes-
  Dodson arousal modulation, ACT-R power-law decay, spacing effect, spreading
  activation, and state-dependent recall.
- **Pre-registered evidence programme.** `preregistration.md` (S1 LoCoMo, S2
  realistic v2) + `preregistration_addendum_v2.md` (Addenda A/B/C) commit
  hypotheses, effect-size targets, and Holm correction *before* results.
- **Claim-ceiling discipline.** `claim_validation_matrix.json` freezes the
  "allowed public wording" per claim and ties each to evidence, limitations,
  and the next study — forcing paper and README to match evidence, not ambition.
- **Reproducibility primitives.** `benchmarks/common/statistics.py` (bootstrap, McNemar,
  Holm), per-run `results.protocol.json`, deterministic seeds, dual embedders
  (hash + `sbert-bge`). The `.dryrun.*` convention in `benchmarks/locomo/`
  gitignores partial runs so they cannot be cited as evidence.
- **Architecture attribution pre-scaffolded.** `benchmarks/appraisal_confound/`
  anticipates the single most obvious reviewer objection ("AFT wins because
  the LLM appraisal leaks affect into the query") and wires a no-LLM keyword-
  appraisal condition to test it. Runner is implemented and awaiting
  execution.
- **One per-challenge result survives Holm.** On the realistic replay
  `semantic_confound` subset (N=30), AFT top1 = 0.73 vs naive_cosine = 0.47,
  Δ = +0.27 [0.10, 0.43], p_adj = 0.006 — the first per-slice AFT advantage
  that survives multiple-testing correction.

## 4. Gaps and risks

Each gap is named, evidence-backed, and ranked by how much it constrains the
public claim ceiling.

**G1 — No human-ecological evidence (blocks Gate 2).**
`benchmarks/human_eval/` has packets, protocol, and a rater-instruction file,
but no `ratings.jsonl`. Claim `models_human_emotional_memory` stays at
`not_established`. Nothing in the corpus shows that humans perceive AFT output
as more coherent or useful than a cosine baseline.

**G2 — LoCoMo run completed; Gate 1 did NOT pass (negative result, now committed).**
`benchmarks/locomo/results.json` and `results.md` committed 2026-04-27.
Full 10-conversation run (1986 QA pairs), both `aft` and `naive_rag`:

| System | N | F1 | BLEU-1 | Judge Acc |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| `aft` | 1540 | 0.168 | 0.169 | 0.279 |
| `naive_rag` | 1540 | 0.271 | 0.263 | 0.441 |

Hypothesis tests (pre-reg S1, Holm-Bonferroni n_bootstrap=2000):
- H1 (F1): Δ = −0.101, p_one = 1.0 → **FAIL**
- H2 (judge_acc): Δ = −0.159, p_one = 1.0 → **FAIL**
- Gate 1: **FAIL**

Interpretation: AFT retrieval is optimised for affective relevance, not factual
QA. On LoCoMo — where the gold answer is an explicit factual statement from a
specific turn — mood-congruent and appraisal-weighted retrieval actively
mis-prioritises emotionally salient but factually irrelevant turns. This is an
honest negative result. The "external benchmark advantage" wording remains
prohibited; `10_scientific_quality_bar.md` status updated accordingly.
Claim `locomo_external_qa_negative` added to `claim_validation_matrix.json`.

**G3 — CLOSED 2026-04-27. Appraisal confound resolved.**
Confirmatory run (seed=1, Addendum D Hd1) committed to
`benchmarks/appraisal_confound/results.confirmatory.{json,md}`.

Results (SBERT, N=100, seed=1):

| System | top1 |
|---|---:|
| `aft_noAppraisal` | 0.780 |
| `naive_cosine` | 0.550 |
| `aft_keyword` | 0.160 |

**Hd1 PASS**: aft_noAppraisal (0.78) > naive_cosine (0.55), Δ=+0.23 [+0.12,
+0.34], d=0.515 — effect replicates with new seed. AFT architecture
(preset affect, no appraisal) reliably outperforms naive cosine.

Gate 3 interpretation: the realistic-replay advantage is attributable to
the AFT retrieval architecture, not to LLM-injected appraisal signals.
`aft_keyword` (0.16) confirms that keyword appraisal degrades preset affect
(same destructive-override finding as G3 discovery and Addendum F).

*Initial study (2026-04-26, seed=42, SBERT, N=100):*
`benchmarks/appraisal_confound/results.json` — Ha2 FAIL (aft_keyword vs
naive_cosine, Δ=−0.39), Hb2 FAIL (|aft_keyword − aft_noAppraisal|=0.62).
Pre-registered hypotheses both failed because keyword appraisal destructively
overrides hand-curated preset affect. The descriptive architecture-attribution
comparison (aft_noAppraisal vs naive_cosine, Δ≈+0.23 [+0.12, +0.33]) was the
signal that motivated Hd1 as the confirmatory re-pre-registration.

SBERT bge-small: AFT top1=0.53 vs naive_cosine=0.33, Δ=+0.205 [0.150,0.265],

|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| semantic_confound | 40 | 0.72 | 0.47 | +0.25 |
| affective_arc | 40 | 0.42 | 0.15 | +0.27 |

| same_topic_distractor | 40 | 0.75 | 0.62 | +0.13 |
| recency_confound | 40 | 0.15 | 0.05 | +0.10 |

Aggregate bootstrap CI excludes 0 on all metrics. G4 is resolved.

**G5 — ✅ CLOSED (2026-04-27): Cross-embedder advantage confirmed.**
v2 cross-embedder results: SBERT Δ=+0.205 (d=0.49); e5-small-v2 Δ=+0.155
(d=0.31). Both embedder classes yield consistent positive advantage with
bootstrap CI excluding 0. The earlier hash-embedder inversion at v1.4 was a
hash-embedder artefact — dense-embedding-conditional scope remains honest, but
the claim now holds on two meaningfully different dense embedder classes.
Results: `benchmarks/realistic/results.v2.{sbert,e5}.{json,md}`.

**G6 — Language coverage. ✓ CLOSED (2026-05-02)**
Italian multilingual slice executed on two embedder configurations: SBERT
`all-MiniLM-L6-v2` (EN-centric) and `intfloat/multilingual-e5-small`. AFT
hit@k advantage is significant on both (p=0.0005 SBERT, p=0.001 me5); delta
is consistent at ≈+0.15–0.16. Cross-embedder robustness documented in
[`12_multilingual_followup.md`](12_multilingual_followup.md) and
`benchmarks/preregistration_addendum_h.md`. Language claims now extend to
"Italian and English conversational text".

**G7 — Dimensional coverage. ✓ CLOSED 2026-05-05**
`CoreAffect` is now 3D PAD (valence × arousal × dominance), shipped in v0.8.2
(commit `8b9ddbe`). `AppraisalVector.coping_potential` populates
`CoreAffect.dominance`; `MoodField` uses dominance directly (no heuristic).
Default dominance = 0.5 when no appraisal engine is active. No study yet tests
whether the dominance dimension adds discriminative power over the 2D baseline —
the omission of a dominance-specific study is now a deliberate choice, disclosed
in §Single affect dimension of the paper.

**G8 — Paper narrative risk. ✓ CLOSED 2026-05-05**
The ceiling-effect framing and the controlled-vs-realistic distinction are now
explicitly stated in `paper/main.tex` §Comparative Benchmark → Results (around

Both the controlled tie (0.80 vs 0.80, Δ=0.00, N=20) and the realistic win

**G9 — Under-benchmarked implemented mechanisms. ✓ CLOSED 2026-04-26**
Dual-path encoding and APE-gated reconsolidation ablations executed
(pre-reg v3, Addendum E; `benchmarks/ablation/results.sbert.{json,md,protocol.json}`).

Results (SBERT, N=100, seed=0):

| Variant | top1 | Δ vs full | Holm p_adj | He verdict |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| `full` | 0.70 | — | — | baseline |
| `no_reconsolidation` | 0.70 | 0.00 | 1.000 | He2 **FAIL** |
| `dual_path` | 0.35 | −0.35 | 0.000 | He1 **FAIL** |

**He2 (no_reconsolidation)**: Δ=0.00, p_adj=1.000 — reconsolidation is
implemented per theory (Nader & Schiller 2000) but does not measurably affect
retrieval on this benchmark. The benchmark may not exercise the conditions that
trigger reconsolidation windows (high APE at retrieval).

**He1 (dual_path)**: Δ=−0.35, p_adj≈0 — as expected (caveat documented in
pre-reg v3): the `KeywordAppraisalEngine` degrades preset affect at the slow-path
elaborate step (same destructive-override finding from G3/Addendum A). He1
measures "no appraisal" vs "deferred bad appraisal" — not the more informative
"does deferral mitigate synchronous keyword destruction?", which requires a
synchronous-keyword baseline. **Addendum F CLOSED (2026-04-27) — see below.**

**Addendum F — Hf1 CLOSED 2026-04-27** (`benchmarks/preregistration_addendum_f.md`)

`aft_keyword_synchronous` variant added: synchronous `KeywordAppraisalEngine` at
encode time, no `elaborate()`. Tests Hf1: `dual_path.top1 > aft_keyword_synchronous.top1`.

Results (SBERT, N=100, seed=0):

| Variant | top1 | Δ vs full | Hf1 |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| `dual_path` | 0.350 | −0.35 | — |
| `aft_keyword_synchronous` | 0.070 | −0.63 | — |
| Δ_Hf1 (dual_path − sync) | — | +0.280 | **PASS** |

**Hf1 PASS**: Deferring keyword appraisal to the slow path reduces destructive
override (0.35 vs 0.07). The LeDoux dual-path architecture provides a measurable
benefit *relative to synchronous keyword appraisal*, even though both are inferior
to no appraisal (preset affect only). Note: `aft_keyword_synchronous` actual=0.07
is slightly below the pre-registered expected range (0.10–0.20); destructive
override was more severe on this call path than anticipated.

## 5. Theory–evidence coherence check

| Layer | Tested by | Coverage |
|---|---|---|
| Core affect (Russell) | fidelity, comparative, realistic | strong |
| Affective momentum | `fidelity/test_momentum.py`, ablation (`no_momentum`) | direct |
| Mood field (Heidegger) | `fidelity/test_mood_congruent.py`, `fidelity/test_mood_adaptive.py`, ablation (`no_mood`) | direct |
| Appraisal (Scherer) | `appraisal_quality/`, ablation (`no_appraisal`), appraisal_confound | scaffolded, thin (LLM-dependent) |
| Resonance (spreading + Hebbian) | `fidelity/test_spreading_activation.py`, ablation (`no_resonance`) | direct |
| Dual-path encoding (LeDoux) | ablation `dual_path` (He1 FAIL; expected) + Addendum F Hf1 PASS (dual_path > synchronous) | closed |
| APE-gated reconsolidation | ablation `no_reconsolidation` (He2 FAIL; benchmark doesn't exercise reconsolidation triggers) | closed (null result) |

All seven layers benchmarked. Dual-path shows null vs full (expected: keyword
appraisal destructive) but Hf1 PASS confirms deferred appraisal outperforms
synchronous — the LeDoux dual-path architecture provides measurable benefit at
the *appraisal-timing* level. Reconsolidation shows null result on this dataset
(triggers not exercised).

## 7. Reviewer anticipation (paper-specific)

Five questions a skeptical arXiv reader will ask, with the strongest
currently-defensible answer and the honest remaining gap.

**Q1 — "Does the advantage come from your architecture or from your LLM
appraisal leaking affect into the query?"**
→ *Current answer:* **Closed (Gate 3, G3, 2026-04-27).** `aft_noAppraisal`
(pure preset affect, no appraisal engine) achieves top1=0.78 vs naive_cosine
0.55, Δ=+0.23 [+0.12, +0.34], d=0.52, seed=1 confirmatory. The advantage
holds without any LLM involvement — it is architectural.

**Q2 — "Does AFT beat cosine on real human conversation, not just on a
benchmark you built?"**
→ *Current answer:* No — LoCoMo S1 ran on 1986 QA pairs and AFT underperformed
naive_rag (F1 0.168 vs 0.271, Gate 1 FAIL). This is an honest negative result:
AFT's affective weighting does not help on factual open-domain conversational QA.
The advantage remains restricted to controlled and realistic affect-oriented
replay benchmarks.
*Gap (updated):* Gate 1 is closed with a negative result. The claim ceiling is
unchanged; LoCoMo advantage wording is still prohibited.

**Q3 — "Do humans actually prefer AFT's output?"**
→ *Current answer:* unknown — human_eval packet is ready, no ratings collected.
*Gap:* Gate 2 (G1).

**Q4 — "Why does AFT tie cosine on your controlled probe but beat it on your
realistic probe?"**
→ *Current answer:* the controlled probe hits a dense-embedder ceiling at
N=20 (both systems at 0.80); the N=200 realistic probe discriminates
(Δ=+0.21, d=0.49, SBERT; Δ=+0.16, d=0.31, e5-small-v2).
*Gap:* ~~stated once in `09_current_evidence.md` but not framed explicitly in
`paper/main.tex` (G8)~~ — **G8 CLOSED 2026-05-05**: explicitly framed in
`paper/main.tex` §Comparative Benchmark → Results.

**Q5 — "Does this work outside English, on a sparse embedder, with the
dominance dimension?"**
→ *Current answer (updated 2026-05-05):*
- **Multilingual**: Italian (G6, Hd2_IT PASS, Δ=+0.163, p=0.012) and Spanish
  (Hd2_ES SBERT PASS, Δ=+0.138, p=0.045) confirmed; me5-small ES borderline
  (Δ=+0.113, p=0.110). Scope extended to "English + Italian + Spanish + dense
  embedding".
- **Sparse embedder** (G5): still scoped out; hash-embedder gap Δ≈+0.06 ns.
  Documented in §Language and embedding scope of the paper.
- **Dominance** (G7): shipped in v0.8.2 — CoreAffect is now 3D PAD. No study
  yet isolates dominance's discriminative contribution.
*Remaining gap:* G5 (sparse embedder), dominance ablation study (no pre-reg yet).
G6 and G7 are closed.
