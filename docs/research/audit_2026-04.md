# Audit: Studies & Research (April 2026)

A critical self-review of the AFT research corpus — strengths, gaps, and
priority order for closing the remaining evidence. Intended both as an internal
compass and as reviewer-anticipation prep for the arXiv submission.

---

## 1. Snapshot

AFT is shipped as a five-layer emotional memory library with a theory stack
(philosophy → neuroscience → computational models → design principles), 9
families of empirical studies, a pre-registration + addendum, and a canonical
claim matrix. The public claim ceiling, per
[`10_scientific_quality_bar.md`](10_scientific_quality_bar.md), is currently
**"strong theory-driven prototype with early controlled evidence."** Of the six
tracked claims in
[`claim_validation_matrix.json`](claim_validation_matrix.json), three have
controlled/early evidence, one carries strong intra-theory evidence, one is
`implemented` only, and one is `not_established`; none carry ecological
validation yet. Three pre-committed quality gates (external benchmark, human
eval, architecture attribution) are all still open.

## 3. Strengths

- **Theory-fidelity density.** 126 cases across 20 phenomena in
  `benchmarks/fidelity/` directly validate Russell mood-congruence, Yerkes-
  Dodson arousal modulation, ACT-R power-law decay, spacing effect, spreading
  activation, and state-dependent recall.
- **Pre-registered evidence programme.** `preregistration.md` (S1 LoCoMo, S2
  realistic v2) + `preregistration_addendum_v2.md` (Addenda A/B/C) commit
  hypotheses, effect-size targets, and Holm correction *before* results.
- **Claim-ceiling discipline.** `claim_validation_matrix.json` freezes the
  "allowed public wording" per claim and ties each to evidence, limitations,
  and the next study — forcing paper and README to match evidence, not ambition.
- **Reproducibility primitives.** `benchmarks/common/statistics.py` (bootstrap, McNemar,
  Holm), per-run `results.protocol.json`, deterministic seeds, dual embedders
  (hash + `sbert-bge`). The `.dryrun.*` convention in `benchmarks/locomo/`
  gitignores partial runs so they cannot be cited as evidence.
- **Architecture attribution pre-scaffolded.** `benchmarks/appraisal_confound/`
  anticipates the single most obvious reviewer objection ("AFT wins because
  the LLM appraisal leaks affect into the query") and wires a no-LLM keyword-
  appraisal condition to test it. Runner is implemented and awaiting
  execution.
- **One per-challenge result survives Holm.** On the realistic replay
  `semantic_confound` subset (N=30), AFT top1 = 0.73 vs naive_cosine = 0.47,
  Δ = +0.27 [0.10, 0.43], p_adj = 0.006 — the first per-slice AFT advantage
  that survives multiple-testing correction.

## 4. Gaps and risks

Each gap is named, evidence-backed, and ranked by how much it constrains the
public claim ceiling.

**G1 — No human-ecological evidence (blocks Gate 2).**
`benchmarks/human_eval/` has packets, protocol, and a rater-instruction file,
but no `ratings.jsonl`. Claim `models_human_emotional_memory` stays at
`not_established`. Nothing in the corpus shows that humans perceive AFT output
as more coherent or useful than a cosine baseline.

**G2 — LoCoMo not yet run on live data (blocks Gate 1).**
The runner is implemented and
[`10_scientific_quality_bar.md`](10_scientific_quality_bar.md) marks it as
"Running as of 2026-04-24", but `benchmarks/locomo/` contains only scaffolding
(runner, adapters, dataset loader, scoring) — no `results.json`. Until Gate 1
clears, no "external benchmark advantage" wording is permitted anywhere in
paper or README.

**G3 — CLOSED 2026-04-27. Appraisal confound resolved.**
Confirmatory run (seed=1, Addendum D Hd1) committed to
`benchmarks/appraisal_confound/results.confirmatory.{json,md}`.

Results (SBERT, N=100, seed=1):

| System | top1 |
|---|---:|
| `aft_noAppraisal` | 0.780 |
| `naive_cosine` | 0.550 |
| `aft_keyword` | 0.160 |

**Hd1 PASS**: aft_noAppraisal (0.78) > naive_cosine (0.55), Δ=+0.23 [+0.12,
+0.34], d=0.515 — effect replicates with new seed. AFT architecture
(preset affect, no appraisal) reliably outperforms naive cosine.

Gate 3 interpretation: the realistic-replay advantage is attributable to
the AFT retrieval architecture, not to LLM-injected appraisal signals.
`aft_keyword` (0.16) confirms that keyword appraisal degrades preset affect
(same destructive-override finding as G3 discovery and Addendum F).

*Initial study (2026-04-26, seed=42, SBERT, N=100):*
`benchmarks/appraisal_confound/results.json` — Ha2 FAIL (aft_keyword vs
naive_cosine, Δ=−0.39), Hb2 FAIL (|aft_keyword − aft_noAppraisal|=0.62).
Pre-registered hypotheses both failed because keyword appraisal destructively
overrides hand-curated preset affect. The descriptive architecture-attribution
comparison (aft_noAppraisal vs naive_cosine, Δ≈+0.23 [+0.12, +0.33]) was the
signal that motivated Hd1 as the confirmatory re-pre-registration.

**G4 — Statistical power is thin outside semantic_confound.**
Realistic replay is N=100 aggregate; per-challenge breakdown in
`challenge_subset_pairwise.json`:

| Challenge | N | AFT top1 | naive top1 | Δ | p_adj (Holm) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| semantic_confound | 30 | 0.73 | 0.47 | +0.27 | **0.006** ✓ |
| affective_arc | 25 | 0.80 | 0.60 | +0.20 | 0.068 |
| same_topic_distractor | 25 | 0.64 | 0.48 | +0.16 | 0.240 |
| recency_confound | 20 | 0.60 | 0.45 | +0.15 | 0.240 |

Only `semantic_confound` survives correction. S2 pre-registers N≥200; the
remaining slices need that larger sample before any per-slice claim lands in
the paper.

**G5 — Embedder-sensitive results.**
Comparative benchmark (SBERT) ties AFT and naive_cosine at recall@5 = 0.80
(N=20, ceiling). The realistic replay gap inverts under the hash embedder:
`semantic_confound` AFT = 0.13 vs naive = 0.07 under hash (no significant
advantage), but 0.73 vs 0.47 under SBERT. The advantage is clearly dense-
embedding-conditional. The paper's scope must therefore say so, or the cross-
embedder slice (Addendum B) must run and hold before broader wording.

**G6 — Language coverage.**
Appraisal prompts, benchmark datasets, and scenario corpora are English-only.
Multilingual slice (Addendum B) is scheduled but unexecuted. Per
[`08_limitations.md`](08_limitations.md), any claim beyond "English
conversational text" is not yet licensed.

**G7 — Dimensional coverage.**
Core affect is operationalized in 2D (valence × arousal); the P of PAD
(dominance) sits on the roadmap at v0.8.0. No study currently tests whether
dominance would add discriminative power — so the omission is a choice, not a
validated simplification.

**G8 — Paper narrative risk.**
`paper/main.tex` presents the 0.70 vs 0.50 realistic number as the headline,
while the comparative benchmark (which the paper also reports) shows AFT and
naive_cosine tied at 0.80 on SBERT. A careful reviewer will ask: "Why does
AFT tie the baseline on the controlled probe but beat it on the realistic
probe?" The answer — that the controlled probe hits a dense-embedder ceiling
at N=20 while the realistic probe discriminates at N=100 — is defensible but
must be stated explicitly in the paper's evaluation section rather than left
to a reviewer to reconstruct.

**G9 — Under-benchmarked implemented mechanisms.**
Dual-path encoding (LeDoux) is implemented via `EmotionalMemory.elaborate()`
(70/30 blend) and APE-gated reconsolidation via the Pearce-Hall gate inside
retrieval. Neither has a dedicated ablation or comparative study. The paper
claims both as contributions, but the ablation suite only isolates appraisal,
mood, momentum, and resonance — not dual-path vs. single-path or reconsolidation
on/off. This is a provenance gap between claim and evidence.

## 5. Theory–evidence coherence check

| Layer | Tested by | Coverage |
|---|---|---|
| Core affect (Russell) | fidelity, comparative, realistic | strong |
| Affective momentum | `fidelity/test_momentum.py`, ablation (`no_momentum`) | direct |
| Mood field (Heidegger) | `fidelity/test_mood_congruent.py`, `fidelity/test_mood_adaptive.py`, ablation (`no_mood`) | direct |
| Appraisal (Scherer) | `appraisal_quality/`, ablation (`no_appraisal`), appraisal_confound | scaffolded, thin (LLM-dependent) |
| Resonance (spreading + Hebbian) | `fidelity/test_spreading_activation.py`, ablation (`no_resonance`) | direct |
| Dual-path encoding (LeDoux) | none — only `elaborate()` unit tests | **gap** |
| APE-gated reconsolidation | fidelity reconsolidation test; no comparative | **gap** |

Five layers are directly benchmarked; two implemented mechanisms that the
paper highlights are not.

## 6. Recommended priority order

Ordered by **evidence-per-cost** and by which gap most constrains the claim
ceiling. Each item is framed as "do X before claiming Y."

1. **Execute `appraisal_confound` (Gate 3). ✓ DONE 2026-04-27.**
   Hd1 PASS: aft_noAppraisal (0.78) > naive_cosine (0.55), Δ=+0.23 with
   new seed. "Architecture drives the advantage" is now claimable.
2. **Execute LoCoMo S1 (Gate 1).** Unlocks any mention of external-benchmark
   validity. Without Gate 1, the paper cannot position AFT as anything
   stronger than "controlled-synthetic advantage."
3. **Ship the human-eval pilot (Gate 2).** Even a minimal N=5 raters × 10
   scenarios lifts claim `models_human_emotional_memory` out of
   `not_established`. Addendum C already specifies Krippendorff α ≥ 0.67 as
   the publishability criterion.
4. **Expand realistic replay to v2, N≥200, with cross-embedder slice.**
   Addresses G4 + G5 together. Until this runs, per-challenge claims beyond
   `semantic_confound` are overclaims, and any sentence about the advantage
   generalizing across embedders is unsupported.
5. **Add a dedicated ablation for dual-path encoding and
   APE-reconsolidation.** Closes G9 so the paper's layer-by-layer
   contribution table is complete rather than selective.
6. **Multilingual slice (Addendum B).** Last — scope-expanding rather than
   scope-defending. Until it runs, keep all language claims pinned to
   "English conversational text."

## 7. Reviewer anticipation (paper-specific)

Five questions a skeptical arXiv reader will ask, with the strongest
currently-defensible answer and the honest remaining gap.

**Q1 — "Does the advantage come from your architecture or from your LLM
appraisal leaking affect into the query?"**
→ *Current answer:* **Closed (Gate 3, G3, 2026-04-27).** `aft_noAppraisal`
(pure preset affect, no appraisal engine) achieves top1=0.78 vs naive_cosine
0.55, Δ=+0.23 [+0.12, +0.34], d=0.52, seed=1 confirmatory. The advantage
holds without any LLM involvement — it is architectural.

**Q2 — "Does AFT beat cosine on real human conversation, not just on a
benchmark you built?"**
→ *Current answer:* not yet shown — LoCoMo runner is ready but unexecuted.
*Gap:* Gate 1 (G2).

**Q3 — "Do humans actually prefer AFT's output?"**
→ *Current answer:* unknown — human_eval packet is ready, no ratings collected.
*Gap:* Gate 2 (G1).

**Q4 — "Why does AFT tie cosine on your controlled probe but beat it on your
realistic probe?"**
→ *Current answer:* the controlled probe hits a dense-embedder ceiling at
N=20 (both systems at 0.80); the N=100 realistic probe discriminates.
*Gap:* stated once in `09_current_evidence.md` but not framed explicitly in
`paper/main.tex` (G8).

**Q5 — "Does this work outside English, on a sparse embedder, with the
dominance dimension?"**
→ *Current answer:* no — scoped to English + dense embedding + 2D circumplex.
*Gap:* G5 + G6 + G7. All three are honestly acknowledged in
[`08_limitations.md`](08_limitations.md), but the paper's scope statement must
repeat this explicitly.
